Madurai is one of the major cities in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It is the administrative headquarters of Madurai District, the third largest city in Tamil Nadu and 25th most populated city in India. Located on the banks of River Vaigai, Madurai has been a major settlement for two millennia. Madurai is closely associated with the Tamil language, and the third Tamil Sangam, a major congregation of Tamil scholars said to have been held in the city. The recorded history of the city goes back to the 3rd century BCE, being mentioned by Megasthenes, the Greek ambassador to the Maurya empire, and Kautilya, a minister of the Mauryan emperor Chandragupta Maurya. Signs of human settlements and Roman trade links dating back to 300BC are evident from excavations by Archeological Survey of India in Manalur. The city is believed to be of significant antiquity and has been ruled, at different times, by the Pandyas, Cholas, Madurai Sultanate, Vijayanagar Empire, Madurai Nayaks, Carnatic kingdom, and the British East India Company British Raj. Rameswaram, (also spelt as Ramesvaram, Rameshwaram) is a town and a second grade municipality in the Ramanathapuram district in the South Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It is on Pamban Island separated from mainland India by the Pamban channel and is about 40 kilometres from Mannar Island, Sri Lanka. It is in the Gulf of Mannar, at the tip of the Indian peninsula. Pamban Island, also known as Rameswaram Island, is connected to mainland India by the Pamban Bridge. Rameswaram is the terminus of the railway line from Chennai and Madurai. Together with Varanasi, it is considered to be one of the holiest places in India to Hindus, and part of the Char Dham pilgrimage.

Day 1
HOMETOWN-TRIVANDRUM

Depart from your hometown and arrive in Trivandrum. Dinner and overnight at Trivandrum.

Day 2
TRIVANDRUM

After breakfast, enjoy the city tour of Trivandrum. Visit Padmanabh Swami Temple, Raja Ravi Varma Art Gallery and Napier Museum (subject to operation). Free time in the evening to have fun at Kovalam beach. Dinner and overnight at Trivandrum.

Day 3
TRIVANDRUM-KANYAKUMARI

After breakfast, proceed to Kanyakumari. En-route visit Kanyakumari wax museum, Visit Kanyakumari Mandir, Tri-Samudra Milan and Gandhi Mandapam. Take a ferry ride to Swami Vivekanand Rock Memorial to see meditation hall and smarak. Dinner and overnight at Kanyakumari.

Day 4
KANYAKUMARI-RAMESHWARAM

After breakfast, proceed to Rameshwaram. On arrival visit Rameshwaram Temple which is one of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines, Dhanushkodi and Pamban Bridge. Dinner and overnight at Rameshwaram.

Day 5
RAMESHWARAM-MADURAI

After breakfast, proceed to Madurai. On arrival visit Meenakshi temple - Madurai's claim to fame for its meticulous colorful carvings on the Gopurams (towers), in the vivid Dravidian image of Gods, Goddess, animal and mythical figures. Dinner and overnight at Madurai.

Day 6
MADURAI-THEKKADY

After breakfast, proceed to Periyar. On arrival visit Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary by boat in Periyar Lake(subject to peration). Enjoy Kairali show in the evening. Dinner and overnight at Periyar.

Day 7
THEKKADY-MUNNAR

After breakfast, enjoy shopping at Kumily Spice Village. Proceed to Munnar. Arrive at Munnar in evening. Dinner and overnight at Munnar.

Day 8
MUNNAR

After breakfast, enjoy speed boat ride at Mattupetty Dam. View Tea Garden, visit Echo Point and Eravikulam (Rajamalai) National Park. Dinner and overnight at Munnar.

Day 9
MUNNAR-COCHIN

After breakfast, proceed to Cochin. On arrival visit St. Francis Church, Jewish Synagogue (subject to operation) and Dutch Palace. Take a Harbour Cruise Ride to see the Chinese Fishing Nets. Dinner and overnight at Cochin.

Day 10
BACK TO HOMETOWN

After breakfast, board the flight or train for your hometown. Tour concludes.
