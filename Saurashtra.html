Saurashtra - 7 Days

Overview
Itinerary
Cost

Saurashtra peninsula is bound on the south and south-west by the Arabian sea, on the north-west by the Gulf of Kutch and on the east by the Gulf of Khambhat. From the apex of these two gulfs, the Little Rann of Kutch and Khambhat, waste tracts half salt morass half sandy desert, stretch inland towards each other and complete the isolation of Kathiawar, except one narrow neck which connects it on the north-east with the mainland of Gujarat. The peninsula is sometimes referred to as Kathiawar after the Kathi Darbar, which once ruled most of the region. However, Saurashtra is not entirely synonymous with Kathiawar, since a small portion of the historical Saurashtra region extends beyond the Kathiawar peninsula. Sorath forms the southern portion of the peninsula."Beyond the gulf of Baraca is that of Barygaza and the coast of the country of Ariaca, which is the beginning of the Kingdom of Nambanus and of all India. That part of it lying inland and adjoining Scythia is called Abiria, but the coast is called Syrastrene. It is a fertile country, yielding wheat and rice and sesame oil and clarified butter, cotton and the Indian cloths made therefrom, of the coarser sorts. Very many cattle are pastured there, and the men are of great stature and black in colour. The metropolis of this country is Minnagara, from which much cotton cloth is brought down to Barygaza.

Day 1
HOMETOWN-RAJKOT

Depart from your hometown to Rajkot.

Day 2
RAJKOT-JAMNAGAR-DWARKA

After breakfast, proceed Jamnagar from Rajkot. On arrival visit Lakhota lake and Lakhota Museum. Visit Bal Hanuman Temple known for its non-stop Ramdhun since 1956 and it mentioned in Guinness book of world records. Also visit Khijadiya Bird Sanctuary and Narara Marin National Park. Later move to Dwarka. Check-in to the hotel in Dwarka. Dinner and overnight stay at Dwarka.

Day 3
DWARKA

After breakfast, proceed to the sacred town of Dwarka. Visit Dwarkadeesh Temple. Take a holy dip in Gomti river, later leave to visit Nageshwar Jyotirling, Gopi Talav, Bet Dwarka and on way back, do visit Ruknnani Temple. Evening visit other temples on coastal area, attend evening aarti at Dwarkadish Temple. Dinner and overnight stay at Dwarka.

Day 4
DWARKA-PORBANDAR-SOMNATH

Early morning, depart to Porbandar. Visit KirtiMandir – the place where Gandhiji was born. Later proceed to Somnath. En-route you can enjoy largest Coastal High Way of Madhavpur, Chorwad to Veraval. On arrival visit BhalkaTirth, TriveniGhat and Somnath Temple. In the evening attend Aarti and later watch light and Sound show. Overnight stay at the hotel in Somnath.

Day 5
SOMNATH-DIU

After breakfast, proceed to Diu. At Diu visit Nagoa Beach, Fort of Diu, Diu Church, local City tour. Dinner and overnight stay in Diu.

Day 6
DIU-SASAN GIR

After breakfast, proceed to Sasan Gir-The One and only Asiatic Lions Home. Leave for morning safari in Gir National Park to spot Asiatic Lions, Leopards, Spotted Deer’s, Peacocks and various species of Birds. Return to hotel and overnight stay at Sasan Gir.

Day 7
JUNAGADH_RAJKOT-BACK TO HOMETOWN

After breakfast, proceed to Junagadh. Visit Uperkot Fort, Ashok Rock Edict, Mahabat Makabara and Junagadh Museum. En-route visit Jalaram Temple at Virpur and Swaminarya Temple, Navlakha Palace and Vintage Car museum at Gondal. Late evening arrival at Rajkot. Drop at Railway Station or Airport. Tour concludes.
