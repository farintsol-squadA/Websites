Nepal - 8 Days

Overview
Itinerary
Cost

Nepal, officially the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal, is a landlocked country in South Asia located mainly in the Himalayas but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. With an estimated population of 26.4 million, it is 48th largest country by population and 93rd largest country by area. It borders China in the north and India in the south, east, and west while Bangladesh is located within only 27 km (17 mi) of its southeastern tip and Bhutan is separated from it by the Indian state of Sikkim. A Himalayan state, Nepal has a diverse geography, including fertile plains, subalpine forested hills, and eight of the world’s ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth. Kathmandu is the nation’s capital and largest city. Nepal is a multiethnic nation with Nepali as the official language. The name "Nepal" is first recorded in texts from the Vedic Age, the era in which Hinduism was founded, the predominant religion of the country. In the middle of the first millennium BCE, Gautama Buddha, the founder of Buddhism, was born in southern Nepal. Parts of northern Nepal were intertwined with the culture of Tibet. The centrally located Kathmandu Valley was the seat of the prosperous Newar confederacy known as Nepal Mandala.

Day 1
HOMETOWN-KATHMANDU

Arrive at Kathmandu airport, report to the coach and proceed to hotel. Dinner and overnight stay at Kathmandu.

Day 2
KATHMANDU-CHITWAN

After breakfast, proceed to Chitwan. Arrive at Chitwan. Enjoy Tharu Cultural dance show in the evening. Dinner and overnight at Chitwan.

Day 3
CHITWAN

After breakfast, visit Chitwan National Park - well known for 'One Horned Rhinoceros' and a shelter to few Royal Bengal Tigers. Enjoy Elephant Bathing (subject to operation). Later enjoy whole day jungle activities such as, elephant ride, canoe ride, village visit. Dinner and overnight at Chitwan.

Day 4
POKHARA

After breakfast, visit Manokamana Temple by rope way. Proceed to Pokhara. During journey enjoy beautiful view of riverbanks and Mt. Fishtail. Arrive at Pokhara in the evening. Visit Bindyabasini Temple. Dinner and overnight at Pokhara.

Day 5
POKHARA

In the morning visit Sarangkot sunrise point. En-route enjoy the view of Annapurna mountain ranges. Later enjoy a pleasant boat ride in Phewa lake - reflecting the panoramic views of Mt. Fishtail on its backdrop and visit Barahi Temple located on an island in Phewa lake and Visit Devi's Fall, Later visit International Mountain Museum (Pokhara Museum) - that reflects an ethnic mosaic of western Nepal. Free time for shopping.

Day 6
POKHARA-KATHMANDU

After breakfast, proceed to Kathmandu by flight. Arrive in Kathmandu. On arrival visit Visit Bhaktapur Darbar Squre - a UNESCO World heritage site includes 55 peacock window mahal from outside and Taleju mata temple. Later visit Budhanilkantha - "Sleeping Vishnu", Boudhanath Stupa - one of the biggest stupas in the world built during 5th century A.D., In the evening enjoy Aarti at Pashupatinath Temple - the holiest Hindu pilgrimage destination in Nepal. Dinner and overnight stay at Kathmandu.

Day 7
KATHMANDU

After breakfast, in the morning guest can enjoy optional activity of Mountain Flight on their own cost. Visit some of the revered monuments of Kathmandu like Swayambhunath Stupa - one of the glorious stupas in the world. En-route enjoy the view of Kathmandu valley. Visit Patan Darbar Square - a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Later visit Patan Kumari Mata Temple, Hiranyan Mahabir Golden Temple and Krishna temple. Dinner and overnight stay at Kathmandu.

Day 8
BACK TO HOMETOWN

Board the flight for your hometown. Tour concludes.
